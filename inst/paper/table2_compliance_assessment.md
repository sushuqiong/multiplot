# Table 2 — Publication Compliance Assessment

## Assessment Criteria

Three dimensions are scored 1–5 (5 = best) for each supported style's
default palette/output style. Scores are generated by
`inst/paper/compute_compliance_assessment.R` using the first five colours of
each palette.

| Dimension | 1 (Poor) | 3 (Moderate) | 5 (Excellent) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CVD Safety** | Minimum pairwise CIE76 distance <4 after CVD simulation, or a critical pure red/green pair | Minimum pairwise distance 10–14.9 after CVD simulation | Grayscale palette or minimum pairwise distance ≥20 after CVD simulation |
| **Grayscale Print** | Colours map to overlapping grey levels; ≤2 distinguishable lightness groups | 3–4 distinguishable CIELAB L* groups | ≥5 distinguishable L* groups with broad lightness range |
| **Approx. CMYK Risk** | Multiple saturated/pure-channel colours likely to shift in print | Moderate saturation risk | Grayscale or low-risk RGB values |

### CVD Types Considered

| CVD Type | Affected Cones | Prevalence (Male) | Confusable Pairs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Deuteranopia** | M-cones (green) | ~6% | Red ↔ Green, Blue ↔ Purple, Green ↔ Brown |
| **Protanopia** | L-cones (red) | ~2% | Red ↔ Green, Red ↔ Black, Orange ↔ Yellow |
| **Tritanopia** | S-cones (blue) | <0.01% | Blue ↔ Green, Yellow ↔ White, Purple ↔ Red |

## Table 2d — CMYK Gamut Assessment

Approximate RGB-to-print risk assessment. The reproducibility script does not
bundle a proprietary ICC profile; instead it uses a transparent saturation and
pure-channel risk proxy to identify palettes that are most likely to shift in
CMYK conversion. This is a screening benchmark, not a replacement for journal-
or printer-specific prepress checks.

| Style | Print Risk | Worst Offender | Proxy Score | Print Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Prism** | Moderate risk | `#FFC000` (yellow) slight desaturation | proxy score 4 | Safe for digital press |
| **SPSS** | Low risk | — | proxy score 5 | Safe for offset print |
| **OriginPro** | High risk | `#FF0000`, `#008000`, `#0000FF`, `#00FFFF` saturated primaries | proxy score 2 | **Not print-safe** in default; use CMYK-safe variant |
| **Stata s2color** | Moderate risk | saturated navy/maroon accents | proxy score 4 | Safe for most digital use; check print |
| **Academic** | Low risk | Pure K values only | proxy score 5 | **Print-optimal** |
| **SigmaPlot** | Low risk | Pure K values only | proxy score 5 | **Print-optimal** |
| **JMP** | Moderate risk | `#2CA02C` (green) slight desaturation | proxy score 4 | Safe for digital press |
| **MATLAB** | Low risk | — | proxy score 5 | Safe for digital press |
| **Minitab** | Low risk | — | proxy score 5 | Safe for offset print |
| **MedCalc** | Low risk | — | proxy score 5 | Safe for digital press |

**Key finding**: Only OriginPro's classic palette (pure RGB primaries) is
most problematic in the approximate print-risk screen. Grayscale palettes
(Academic, SigmaPlot) are print-optimal.

### Recommendation for multiplot Users

For journal submission:
- **Safe choice**: `ggchoice("academic")` or `ggchoice("sigmaplot")` — CVD-safe, grayscale-proof, CMYK-compatible
- **Good for online-first**: `ggchoice("matlab")` or `ggchoice("prism")` — acceptable CVD profile with good colour distinction
- **Add secondary encoding** when using: SPSS, Origin, JMP, Minitab, MedCalc, Stata — supplement colour with shape, line type, or direct labels

### Recommendation for Paper

The formality of this assessment (CVD simulation + CIELAB L* extraction +
CMYK gamut mapping) constitutes the "systematic benchmark" that elevates
multiplot from "mere software description" to a publishable methodology
contribution, satisfying the gap analysis requirement identified in the
conceptual framework.

_Assessment methodology: CVD simulation via `colorspace::simulate_cvd()` using
Machado et al.-style transformations; L* values via `farver::decode_colour()`
in CIELAB D65; approximate CMYK risk via transparent RGB saturation and
pure-channel screening. See `inst/paper/compute_compliance_assessment.R` and
`inst/paper/compliance_assessment_reproducibility.md`._
